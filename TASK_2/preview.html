Hook Bank (5 Hooks)

01
Pain Point Hook
"I was tired of spending ₹3,000 on something that looked cheap online."
Calls out budget frustration + quality disappointment. Relatable before the brand appears.
02
Curiosity Hook
"Okay so I found a clothing store in Hyderabad that nobody's talking about yet."
"Nobody's talking about yet" triggers discovery instinct. Viewer feels they're getting insider info.
03
Social Proof Hook
"Three of my friends asked where I got this outfit within the same day."
Real-world social signal — not a brand claim. Audience immediately wants what caused that reaction.
04
Contrast Hook
"I used to avoid college fests because I never had anything to wear. Until this."
Before → turning point structure. "Until this" creates a visual cliff-hanger — they keep watching.
05
Bold Claim Hook
"Affordable and actually stylish isn't supposed to exist in the same sentence."
Challenges a widely held belief. Viewer agrees or is curious to prove it wrong — either way, they stay.

Identity / Aspiration Angle
Bold Claim Hook Facebook Reels ~45 sec

"Affordable and actually stylish isn't supposed to exist in the same sentence."
Problem 3–10 sec
"That's what I believed for a long time. Like, you either spend a lot and look good, or you save money and settle. And when you're in your early twenties just starting out in Hyderabad — a job, rent, going out, all of it adding up — fashion is usually the first thing that gets cut."

"Threads & Thread Co. kind of broke that rule for me. It's this local brand here in Hyderabad — they've got streetwear, fusion, occasion stuff — that actually looks like it has some thought behind it. And the pricing is genuinely honest."
Proof 25–38 sec
