## 🎣 5 SCROLL-STOPPING HOOKS

---

### Hook 1 — Pain Point Hook
> *"I was tired of spending ₹3,000 on something that looked cheap online."*

**Why it works:** Immediately calls out the budget frustration + quality disappointment combo that every young Hyderabadi shopper has felt. Relatable before the brand even appears.

---

### Hook 2 — Curiosity Hook
> *"Okay so I found a clothing store in Hyderabad that nobody's talking about yet."*

**Why it works:** "Nobody's talking about yet" triggers the discovery instinct. The viewer feels like they're about to get insider information — they can't scroll away.

---

### Hook 3 — Social Proof Hook
> *"Three of my friends asked where I got this outfit within the same day."*

**Why it works:** Leads with a real-world social proof signal — not a brand claim, but a human moment. The audience immediately wants what caused that reaction.

---

### Hook 4 — Contrast Hook
> *"I used to avoid college fests because I never had anything to wear. Until this."*

**Why it works:** The "before → turning point" structure creates instant emotional investment. The word "this" creates a visual cliff-hanger — they have to keep watching to see what changed.

---

### Hook 5 — Bold Claim Hook
> *"Affordable and actually stylish isn't supposed to exist in the same sentence."*

**Why it works:** Challenges a widely held belief. The viewer either agrees (and wants to know the exception) or is curious enough to prove it wrong. Either way, they keep watching.

---
