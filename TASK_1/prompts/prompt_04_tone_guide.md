# Prompt 04 — Tone Adaptation Guide

## 🎯 Purpose
This guide shows how to adapt the same prompt framework to different local business types by changing the tone instruction.

---

## 🎨 Tone Library

### ☕ Café / Restaurant
```
Tone: Warm, sensory, inviting. Write like a friendly barista who knows your order.
Avoid: Corporate language, food clichés like "mouth-watering" or "finger-licking"
Use: Sensory details (aroma, texture, atmosphere), community feel, local references
```

### 💇 Salon / Beauty Studio
```
Tone: Confident, glamorous but approachable. Like a stylish friend giving honest advice.
Avoid: Overpromising ("transform your look"), generic beauty words
Use: Before/after language, self-care framing, specific service outcomes
```

### 🏥 Clinic / Diagnostic Center
```
Tone: Professional, calm, reassuring. Like a doctor who takes time to explain things clearly.
Avoid: Fear-based language, medical jargon, overly technical terms
Use: Trust signals, patient-first language, clarity and simplicity
```

### 📚 Coaching Institute
```
Tone: Motivational, results-focused, direct. Like a mentor who believes in you.
Avoid: Vague promises ("unlock your potential"), pressure tactics
Use: Specific outcomes (scores, placements, skill names), student success framing
```

### 💼 Agency / Freelancer
```
Tone: Bold, confident, business-focused. Like a partner who gets things done.
Avoid: Buzzwords ("synergy", "leverage", "holistic approach")
Use: ROI language, specific deliverables, client results
```

---

## 🔁 Universal Tone Addition to Any Prompt

Add this to any prompt for better results:

```
Additional tone rules:
- Write in second person ("you", "your") to speak directly to the reader
- Use short sentences for CTAs, longer ones for descriptions
- Every paragraph should end with either a benefit or an emotion
- Avoid starting sentences with "We" — start with "You" or the benefit instead
```

## 📊 Tone Comparison Example

**Generic (bad):** "We are a café that serves coffee and snacks in Bangalore."

**Brew & Bloom tone (good):** "Your next favourite corner of Bangalore — where single-origin coffee meets an afternoon worth staying for."

**Clinic tone (same business type, different tone):** "Clear answers, gentle care — because your health deserves more than a rushed appointment."
